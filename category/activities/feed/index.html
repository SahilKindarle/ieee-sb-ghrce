Date: 6th August, 2016

Venue: Platform Gyan Mandir School

Time: 3:00pm to 5:35pm

In today’s economic and social environment, issues related to social responsibility and sustainability are gaining more and more importance. Academic goals are inseparable from the societies and environments within which they operate. Whilst short-term economic gain can be pursued, the failure to account for longer-term social and environmental impacts makes those academic practices unsustainable.

Platform Gyan Mandir School founded in June 2010 is affiliated by Nagpur Municipal Corporation, Indian Railways and Nagpur Police. The school’s main objective is to give shelter to vagrant kids and provide proper education to them. There are in total 36 children in the vicinity form 1st grade to those pursuing Engineering. The school provides proper education to the students as well.

 

IEEE’s objective was to inform the students about IEEE and what work it does and make the students enjoy the time with us. We gave an introduction to the students about IEEE, the work IEEE does to make lives better for those who use technology and those who are not fortunate enough to use one, we had a great interactive session with them.

Second, we had planned to make these kids enjoy so that they will forget their home for some time being with us. Well, there were many talented children there. Some of them knew how to break dance, some of them liked to sing and we found a karate player as well.

 

As we continued our interaction with the students we came across some disheartening stories of how the children had suffered in their short lifespan.

One of them, at the age of 10 was forced to work in a bangle making factory at Jaipur, he detested it.  When he tried to escape from the misery, the authorities attacked him by sticking pieces of glass on his face. That day he ran from the city and started his journey towards Mumbai and in the end ending up in Nagpur.

Another demoralizing story was of a student who lost his parents on a railway station and all of his credentials were stolen. Nagpur’s Railway authority then sent him at this school to make his life better.

There were kids from 1st standard who had some or the other tragedies in their life. Some had lost their family members, some treated with cruelty, some getting lost from their family and the list goes on.

 

Then we found a poet amongst the students who was in Engineering 2nd year computer Science branch in Gurunanak Engineering College. He iterated a well written poem in Marathi to us on ‘college life’.

   

 

                    

Three kids from Assam taught us Assamese as well.

Filled with joy the students of the school wrote the thankyou letter by themselves. The Head Master said something which brightened our day even more, he said, “What is the use of taking photos with me, take photos with the kids. We are just their helpers, nothing more. They need some love, we are trying to get them out of their inferior self and bring out their talents which are hidden in their nutshell. These kids have been through a lot in their small lifetime, we are trying to educate them through our system an make their life better.”

                 

 

We ended our event by giving thme gifts, which included pens, pencils and perks. The kids continued requested us to come soon which we acknowledged. We bid them and the Head Master farewell with many memories installed.

Date:28 july 2016

Day:Thursday

                  A debate competition was organised by IEEE SB 62361 on 28 july at 3.30 pm on the topic “Advancement in technology- creating job opportunities or unemployment”. The total number of participants  was 33. The event started with the welcoming of judges. The first judge was Prof. Rutika Meshkar ma’am who was welcomed by Ms. Alisha Warke(Vice chair) and the second judge was Dr. Shabbir Zakerya and he was welcomed by Mr. Kirtiraj R. Garud by tulsi saplings.

Bhagyashree Vaidya(exicom) was the host for the event. The event commenced with the introduction to IEEE by Mr. Kirtiraj Garud. In the first round of the event the participants presented their viewson the topic. The qualified 14 students in the second round had perfomed one-on-one debate. On the basis of their content,body language, fluency in english, etc. the winners were selected. Rohit Poduval (ETC 4th year)and Yuvraj Singh (ETC 1st year) were the winners.

The trophies and certificates for the participants were sponsored by “Rainbow Dimensions” startup from GHRCE.

Rutika Meshkar in her speech said that it was the first brach organising such an event inclusive of the participants from all the years and having a very good response and that she would be looking forward for more such events which will give the participants an opportunity to enchance their skills and boost up confidence in them.

The event concluded by the vote of thanks given by Alisha Warke.
